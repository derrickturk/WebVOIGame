The Two Guy VOI Game

God flips a coin with a 30.00% chance of “heads”.

If He gets heads, He assigns a payout drawn from a log-normal distribution with a mean of $500.00 and a P10/P90 ratio of 3.00.

God offers to sell you the payout, if any, at a cost of $200.00.

God's brother, Bob, approaches you and offers yet another deal: for a cost of $10.00, he will peek at God's coin and tell you what he sees before you have to make your decision. However, Bob is a known liar: in fact he tells the truth only 60.00% of the time.

Finally, God's niece, Sally, approaches you and offers a final deal: if and only if you first accept the deal of her father, Bob, Sally will also peek at God's coin and relay her findings before you make your final decision. Sally asks a fee of $10.00, but the apple doesn't fall far from the tree: she tells the truth only 90.00% of the time.

If you accept both Bob and Sally's offers:

On average, you will achieve a net payout of $0.00.

You'll lose money about 0.00% of the time.

You'll lose a bunch of money (more than $100) about 0.00% of the time.

You'll gain a bunch of money (more than $1000) about 0.00% of the time.
